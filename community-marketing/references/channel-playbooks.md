# Channel Playbooks

Use this reference to adapt the same marketing intent to different communities without sounding copied or forced.

## Selection

Prioritize communities where the audience already:

- Asks for the problem your product solves
- Shares workflows, templates, or recommendations
- Has an active moderation culture and recent activity
- Lets you participate without immediate link-dropping

Avoid communities that are:

- Mostly low-signal self-promotion
- Inactive or full of automated reposts
- Misaligned with the product stage or audience maturity

## Post Structure

Use this order for most community posts:

1. Context
2. Specific problem
3. Useful insight, proof, or example
4. One small ask

Keep the ask narrow. Examples:

- "Would you sanity-check this approach?"
- "What would you change?"
- "If useful, I can share the template."
- "Has anyone solved this differently?"

## Channel Rules

### Reddit and forums

- Write like a peer, not a brand.
- Lead with the problem or lesson, not the product.
- Expect direct criticism and answer it concretely.
- Use comments to clarify and add value after the post.

### Slack and Discord communities

- Read the channel norms before posting.
- Prefer helpful replies over standalone announcements.
- DM only when the group norm supports it or a direct intro is invited.
- Use short, conversational messages.

### LinkedIn and X

- Use specific opinions, observations, or lessons.
- Tie the post to a workflow, result, or example.
- Avoid generic thought-leadership phrasing.
- Reply quickly to early comments to keep momentum.

### Ambassador and advocate programs

- Give advocates a clear reason to participate.
- Provide talking points, examples, and guardrails.
- Reward useful actions, not just volume.
- Track who is actually influencing conversations.

## Measurement

Measure by stage:

- Awareness: impressions from the right audience, profile visits, follows from relevant accounts
- Engagement: replies, quote posts, meaningful comments, saves
- Demand: qualified clicks, signup rate, demo requests, direct inquiries
- Research: repeated pain points, objections, language that can be reused in copy

Prefer a simple sheet with:

- Community name
- Date
- Participation mode
- Post or reply link
- Outcome
- Notes on audience language and objections
