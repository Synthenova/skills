# Community Marketing

## Overview

Treat community marketing as an operating system, not a one-off post. Pick the right communities, earn trust before asking for attention, and optimize for signal from real practitioners rather than vanity impressions.

## Workflow

1. Define the goal: awareness, pipeline, activation, retention, referrals, or research.
2. Identify the audience and the specific communities where they already ask for help.
3. Choose the engagement mode:
   - Listen and learn
   - Answer questions
   - Seed a useful discussion
   - Share proof or a resource
   - Recruit advocates or moderators
4. Draft the message for the channel and intent.
5. Check the message against the channel rules in [channel-playbooks.md](references/channel-playbooks.md).
6. Publish, respond quickly, and follow the thread until it cools.
7. Measure quality over volume: replies, saves, qualified clicks, signups, demos, intro calls, or research insights.

## Default Rules

- Lead with usefulness, not promotion.
- Match the tone and norms of the community before adding brand language.
- Use one clear ask per post.
- Avoid recycling the same announcement across channels.
- Prefer proof, examples, and specific lessons over generic hype.
- If a community punishes promotion, participate as a member first and a marketer second.

## Deliverables

When asked to plan or execute community marketing, return:

- Target communities and why they fit
- Recommended participation mode for each community
- Draft post, reply, or DM copy
- Risks or moderation concerns
- Success metrics and a simple tracking plan
