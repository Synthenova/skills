# Judging Scorecard

Use fresh blind judges so the final choice does not inherit the critic's bias.

## Panel Rules

- Judges see only the candidate texts and the task goal.
- Judges do not see the critic rationale or synthesis instructions.
- Candidates stay unlabeled until scoring is complete.
- Score each option independently before ranking them.

## Borda Count

Assign rank-based points for each judge:

- 1st place: 3 points
- 2nd place: 2 points
- 3rd place: 1 point

Add scores across judges and promote the top scorer.

## What Judges Should Penalize

- Generic claims a competitor could say word for word
- Weak or fuzzy differentiation
- Hooks that exist without making a clear promise
- Voice that sounds interchangeable across brands
- Claims that are not supported by the knowledge layer when evidence is available

## What Judges Should Reward

- Specificity
- Distinctiveness
- Clear customer payoff
- Tight, memorable phrasing
- Consistency with proven performance and brand voice

## Tie-Break Rule

If brand voice and evidence point in different directions, use this order:

1. Prefer the candidate backed by the strongest direct evidence from the knowledge layer.
2. Preserve brand voice in the phrasing and tone of that candidate.
3. If evidence support is effectively equal, choose the more on-brand option.
4. If evidence is weak, stale, or indirect, let brand voice decide.
